Overview

Course Description

IGSL students are trained to be servant-steward leaders who are biblically rooted Christian thinkers and contextually reflective practitioners for the purpose of helping fulfill the Great Commission. They are leaders and Bible teachers who need to continuously grow in their knowledge of Scripture, Christ-like character, and leadership skills.


This course builds on the exegetical and homiletical foundations established in previous preaching courses. Students will learn advanced approaches to preaching with particular emphasis on preaching the parables of Jesus and persuasive preaching on contemporary issues. The course seeks to develop both biblical faithfulness and rhetorical effectiveness so that students can communicate God's truth in ways that lead to transformation.

Objectives

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will have demonstrated growth in the following areas:

Knowledge

  • Analyzed audiences and planned effective preaching strategies appropriate to their needs and contexts.
  • Identified principles for making biblical messages relevant to contemporary audiences.
  • Applied sound exegetical and homiletical principles in preaching parables.
  • Explained the dynamics of persuasion and their application to preaching.
  • Evaluated contemporary issues from a biblical perspective and developed persuasive sermons addressing them.

Character

  • Demonstrated commitment to careful interpretation and communication of Scripture.
  • Demonstrated integrity as communicators who seek to model the truth they preach.
  • Demonstrated commitment to relevant preaching through thoughtful audience analysis.
  • Demonstrated commitment to preaching for life transformation rather than merely information transfer.

Skills

  • Prepared and delivered biblically faithful sermons on the parables of Jesus.
  • Prepared and delivered persuasive sermons addressing contemporary issues from a biblical perspective.
  • Evaluated sermons using established criteria for parable and persuasive preaching.
  • Demonstrated increased confidence and effectiveness in sermon delivery.
Readings

Course Readings

Required

Eighteen assigned articles available through Canvas. Students are expected to read all assigned articles before the scheduled class session. Approximately two articles will be assigned for each instructional week.

Suggested

  • Blomberg, Craig. Preaching the Parables: From Responsible Interpretation to Powerful Proclamation.
  • Chapell, Bryan. Christ-Centered Preaching.
  • Keller, Timothy. Preaching: Communicating Faith in an Age of Skepticism.
  • Stanley, Andy and Lane Jones. Communicating for a Change.
  • Willhite, Keith. Preaching with Relevance without Dumbing Down.
Requirements

Course Requirements

A. Reading Reflections 20%

Students will read eighteen assigned articles and submit nine reading reflections through Canvas (approximately one reflection for every two assigned articles). Each reflection should be approximately 300–500 words and address the following questions:

  • What is the most significant insight you gained from the readings?
  • What idea challenged, confirmed, or expanded your understanding of preaching?
  • How might these insights influence your own preaching ministry?

Reflections must be submitted before class.

B. Parables Preaching Project 35%

Each student will prepare and preach a sermon based on one of the parables of Jesus. Students will preach a 20–25 minute sermon during the Parable Preaching Lab.

  • Parables Exegetical Worksheet
  • Parables Homiletical Worksheet
  • Parables Preaching Outline Worksheet
  • Parable Sermon Delivery
C. Persuasive Preaching Project 35%

Each student will prepare and preach a persuasive sermon addressing a contemporary issue from a biblical perspective. Students will preach a 20–25 minute sermon during the Persuasive Preaching Lab.

  • Persuasive Issue Analysis Worksheet
  • Persuasive Biblical and Homiletical Worksheet
  • Persuasive Sermon Outline Worksheet
  • Persuasive Sermon Delivery
D. Participation and Sermon Evaluations 10%

Students are expected to participate actively in discussions, workshops, and preaching labs. Students will complete peer evaluations and self-evaluations for both sermons.
